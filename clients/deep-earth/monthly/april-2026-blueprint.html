Account Diagnosis

Where Things Stand

01

No Email History

This list has never been mailed. There is zero sending reputation with this audience, which means ISPs have no baseline for whether these recipients want Deep Earth's emails. We must warm gradually or risk immediate spam folder placement.

02

Cold List, Unknown Quality

Contacts came from Meta ads and an industry database. Some opted in willingly, others were appended. We do not know bounce rates, spam trap density, or how many addresses are still active. First sends will reveal list health. Expect higher-than-normal bounces on send one.

03

Authentication Unknown

SPF, DKIM, and DMARC records need to be verified in GHL before the first send. If authentication is incomplete, emails will land in spam regardless of content quality. This is a non-negotiable pre-launch checklist item.

04

No Segments

All contacts are in a single unsegmented list. We need to create source-based segments (Meta ad leads vs. database contacts), engagement-based segments (openers vs. non-openers after first sends), and behavioral segments (audit completers, link clickers). Segments get built as data comes in over the first two weeks.

05

GoHighLevel Platform

GHL handles email, automations, and CRM in one place. Merge tag format is {{contact.first_name}}. Automation triggers use tags and workflow rules. All campaigns and flows in this blueprint are designed for GHL's native builder.

Monthly Strategy

The April Approach

Warm Gradually

Send to the smallest, highest-quality segment first (Meta ad opt-ins). Monitor bounces, spam complaints, and open rates before expanding to the full list. If open rates stay above 20% and spam complaints stay below 0.1%, we widen the audience each week.

Trust Before Asking

Every campaign follows the Gain/Logic/Fear sequence. Email 1 paints a picture of what's possible. Email 2 provides the proof and mechanism. Email 3 introduces the cost of inaction. No one gets a hard sell until they have received at least two value deposits.

Establish Sender Reputation

ISPs judge new senders harshly. Consistent sends on a Tuesday/Thursday/Friday cadence, clean list hygiene, and high engagement rates in weeks one and two will build the reputation needed to scale volume in month two.

Build Infrastructure

While campaigns run, we set up three automation flows (Welcome, Post-Audit Follow-Up, Re-engagement) that will continue working between campaigns. These flows handle the contacts who raise their hand, complete an audit, or go quiet.

Deliverability

Do This Before Sending

Cold lists are high-risk for deliverability. These items must be completed before the first campaign goes out.

Confirm Before Launch

  • SPF record includes GHL sending IPs
  • DKIM signing enabled on sending domain
  • DMARC policy set (at minimum p=none for monitoring)
  • Custom tracking domain configured in GHL
  • Reply-to address goes to a monitored inbox

Action Required

  • Run the full list through a verification service (NeverBounce, ZeroBounce) before any sends to remove invalid addresses
  • Remove role-based addresses (info@, admin@, sales@) from the marketing list
  • Set up a Google Postmaster Tools account to monitor domain reputation after first sends
  • Create the suppression smart lists (hard bounces, spam complainers) and exclude from all campaigns

Offers and Risk Reversal

What We're Putting on the Table

Free 12-Point Audit

The primary CTA across all campaigns and automations. A 15-minute audit that reviews ads, SEO, reviews, follow-up, competitive positioning, and more. No obligation, no pitch. This is Deep Earth's top-of-funnel conversion event.

Minute-to-Minute Positioning

Tim's "I don't care if the contract's minute-to-minute" positioning removes the biggest objection (long-term lock-in). This should be reinforced on the strategy call and in Campaign 3. It's a trust accelerator.

Bridal Season Strategy Session

A seasonal variant of the audit specifically for stores preparing for bridal season. Use in Campaign 4 and as a follow-up offer for engaged contacts who didn't convert on the general audit.
