Volume Ramping Protocol

Never increase send volume by more than 2-3x between sends. Even on Klaviyo's shared IP pools, sudden volume spikes can trigger deliverability issues — especially on dedicated sending domains.

Before scheduling campaigns, check segment sizes:

• Campaign 1: Previous Buyers → est. [X] contacts
• Campaign 2: Engaged Non-Buyers → est. [Y] contacts
• Campaign 3: All Active 90 Days → est. [Z] contacts
• Campaign 4: Engaged 60 Days → est. [W] contacts

If any campaign's segment is 3x+ larger than the previous one, add a ramp send between them.

How to split segments in Klaviyo:

1. Go to Lists & Segments → Create Segment
2. Name it "[Segment Name] - Ramp Batch"
3. Conditions: "Date Added > [recent date]" AND "In Segment [target segment]"
4. This gives you the newer/more engaged portion of the segment
5. Send ramp campaign to this smaller group
6. Send full campaign to complete segment on next scheduled send
7. Archive the temporary ramp segment when done

Example: If Campaign 1 targets Previous Buyers (est. 2,000) and Campaign 3 targets All Active 90 Days (est. 15,000), that's a 7.5x jump. Split: send Campaign 2 to Engaged Non-Buyers (~5,000) as a natural ramp, then Campaign 3 to full Active 90 Days.

Note: Klaviyo is generally more forgiving than MailerLite or GHL on volume spikes because of shared IP pools. But the risk increases significantly on dedicated sending domains, and best practice is to ramp regardless.

If the account gets flagged:

• Don't panic — review flags are usually temporary (24-72 hours)
• Pause scheduled sends until cleared
• Resume with smaller audience and ramp properly
