Monthly Strategy

The April Approach

01

Ride the Calendar

April has four natural buying triggers: quinceañera season kickoff, graduation gift shopping, early Mother's Day planning, and everyday Cuban Link demand. Instead of manufacturing urgency, we're meeting people where they already are. Every campaign ties to a real moment their family is already thinking about.

02

Deposit-Deposit-Ask-Convert

Week 1 is a cultural value deposit (quinceañera traditions). Week 2 is a gift guide value deposit (graduation). Week 3 is a soft ask (Mother's Day early access). Week 4 is a conversion ask (Cuban Link spotlight). Each week earns the next level of trust.

03

Segment Smarter

April introduces audience-specific targeting. Campaign 1 goes to past buyers and the Latino community segment. Campaign 4 targets Cuban Link and gold chain buyers specifically. Campaigns 2 and 3 use broader active lists. Build behavioral segments from April click data as it comes in.

04

Target Metrics

Open rate target: 25%+ (baseline month, establishing benchmarks). Click rate target: 2%+. Unsubscribe ceiling: 0.3% per send. Revenue attribution: track in-store mentions of email campaigns and use GHL pipeline tracking for any online inquiries. Every metric this month becomes the data we optimize against.

Month 1 Health Check

This is the baseline month. Suppress inactive contacts, establish consistent sending, and collect the data that will drive every decision going forward.

Do This First (Week 1)

  • 130K+ inactive contacts suppressed
  • Active 90 Days smart list live and sending
  • SPF/DKIM/DMARC configured
  • Language segments (English/Spanish) active
  • Reply-to going to monitored inbox
  • "We Buy Gold" blasts stopped

April Actions

  • Monitor bounce rate after first sends, if hard bounces exceed 1%, clean the list further
  • Check spam complaint rate in GHL, anything above 0.08% needs investigation
  • Run blacklist check on sending domain before first campaign
  • After Week 2, evaluate Active 90 window: if opens are below 15%, tighten to 60 days
  • Build behavioral segments from April click data (Cuban Link Interest, Engagement Ring Interest)

Volume Management

Warmup is complete, but volume discipline is permanent. Monitor volume jumps between campaigns — the 2–3x rule still applies. Even with a healthy sender reputation, sudden volume spikes trigger spam filters.

Campaign Volume Check (Verify Before Scheduling)

  • Campaign 1 targets [segment] → est. [X] contacts
  • Campaign 2 targets [segment] → est. [Y] contacts
  • If Y > 3x X, add a ramp send between them

Warning: Even with a warmed-up domain, jumping from a 500-person segment to a 12,000-person segment in one send WILL trigger flags. Ramp gradually.

GHL

How to Split Segments in GHL

  1. Go to Contacts → Smart Lists → Create Smart List
  2. Name it "[Segment Name] - Ramp Batch"
  3. Filter: "Last Email Activity" is within last 30 days AND is in your target segment
  4. Send to this smaller group first, then expand to full segment next send
  5. Delete temporary Smart Lists after ramping is complete

Testing Strategy

April Split Tests

Each campaign has A/B subject line tests built in. April also introduces an English vs Spanish test to optimize bilingual strategy.

01

Emotional vs. Specific (Quinceañera)

Campaign 1: Subject A ("Her quinceañera deserves the perfect piece") vs Subject D ("Tiaras, gold bracelets, and the traditions that matter"). Hypothesis: the emotional hook wins for this culturally resonant audience because it connects to identity, not just products.

02

Aspirational vs. Practical (Graduation)

Campaign 2: Subject A ("Every milestone deserves gold") vs Subject B ("The graduation gift they'll actually keep"). Hypothesis: practical wins for gift buyers because they're solving a problem ("what do I get them?"), not browsing for themselves.

03

Emotional Hook vs. Urgency (Mother's Day)

Campaign 3: Subject A ("She's been waiting, she just hasn't told you") vs Subject C ("26 days until she expects something beautiful"). Hypothesis: emotional wins because engaged buyers respond to empathy, not countdown pressure.

04

English vs. Spanish Subject Lines

Cross-campaign: For bilingual contacts (tagged as both English and Spanish capable), test Spanish subject lines with English body copy vs all-English. If Spanish subjects outperform by 5%+ on open rate, consider Spanish-first subject lines as the default for this segment going forward. Track this across all four campaigns.

How to run in GHL: When creating each campaign, use the A/B split test feature. Set a 50/50 split. Send the test to 30% of the list first, wait 2 hours, then send the winner to the remaining 70%. Track open rates to determine the winner. Log all results in the performance log for May planning.
