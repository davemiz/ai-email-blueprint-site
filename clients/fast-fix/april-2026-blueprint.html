This Month's Trust Strategy
Month 1:

Trust Recovery

This is Month 1. Fast Fix has never had a strategic email program, so we're building from scratch. The list has ~8,512 active subscribers across 4 locations, but most of them haven't received consistent, value-forward emails before. April is the perfect month to start: Mother's Day (May 11) gives us a natural buying window, graduation season opens up engraving, and spring is when people pull out jewelry they haven't worn in months. The strategy this month: lead with value, give people a reason to visit, and establish the rhythm. Every campaign deposits trust first, then earns the right to ask. The rhythm: value + reason to visit, gift guide, targeted offer, urgency + case spotlight.

Monthly Strategy

The April Approach

01

Primary Goal

Drive foot traffic and fine jewelry case sales through seasonal buying moments (Mother's Day, graduation). Every email should give subscribers a specific reason to visit their local Fast Fix this month.

02

Trust Architecture

The first two campaigns deposit trust, the last two withdraw carefully. Campaign 1 is a value deposit with a soft visit nudge. Campaign 2 is a gift guide (value + consideration). Campaign 3 targets past repair customers with a specific offer. Campaign 4 combines urgency with a case spotlight for the conversion ask.

03

Segmentation

Continue sending by location using Active 90-day lists. Campaign 3 narrows to past repair customers. Campaign 4 narrows to engaged subscribers who opened an email this month. Tighter targeting = higher conversion.

04

Key Metrics

Target open rate: 28%+ on Active 90 sends. Target click rate: 4%+. Unsubscribe ceiling: 0.3% per send. Track foot traffic mentions and fine jewelry case inquiries per location.

Deliverability

Volume Ramping Protocol

The Rule: Never increase send volume by more than 2-3x between sends. GHL, Gmail, Yahoo, and Outlook all flag accounts when daily volume spikes 3x+ from recent history — regardless of engagement quality.

How to check: Before scheduling any campaign, compare the target segment size to your last send volume. If the jump is 3x or more, split the send.

How to Split Segments in GHL

01

Create a Temporary Smart List

Go to Contacts → Smart Lists → Create Smart List. Name it "[Segment Name] - Ramp Batch 1"

02

Filter for Most Engaged

Filter: "Last Email Activity" is within last 30 days AND is in your target segment (e.g., "Active 90 Days - Barton Creek"). This gives you your most engaged contacts — send to them first.

03

Send to Full Segment Next

On the following send, use the full target segment. The initial smaller send warms the domain reputation with high-engagement contacts.

04

Clean Up

Delete the temporary Smart List after ramping is done. It was only needed for the volume transition.

Volume Ramp Reference

Ramp Examples

  • Last send ~1,000 → next target ~3,500: send to ~1,000-1,500 first, then full 3,500
  • Last send ~500 → next target ~2,000: send to ~1,000 first, then full 2,000

If the Account Gets Flagged

  • Don't panic — review flags are usually temporary (24-72 hours)
  • Pause scheduled sends until cleared
  • Resume with smaller audience and ramp properly
  • Contact ESP support with engagement stats if needed

Testing Strategy

April Split Tests

Every campaign has A/B subject line tests baked in. This is the first month, so every test establishes a baseline we'll optimize against going forward.

01

Curiosity vs. Specific Offer

Campaign 1 (Spring Refresh): Subject A ("That necklace deserves a comeback") vs Subject B ("Free quick-clean while you shop"). Hypothesis: the specific offer wins for a service-based email, but curiosity may drive higher opens.

02

Price Anchor vs. Pain Hook

Campaign 2 (Gift Guide): Subject A ("3 Mother's Day ideas, all under $200") vs Subject C ("She doesn't want another candle"). Hypothesis: the price anchor gives a concrete reason to open, but the pain hook is more emotionally engaging.

03

Benefit vs. Emotional Outcome

Campaign 3 (Graduation Engraving): Subject A ("Make their graduation gift personal") vs Subject C ("The grad gift they'll actually keep"). Hypothesis: emotional outcome wins with a warm audience (past customers).

04

Urgency vs. Product Curiosity

Campaign 4 (Last Call): Subject A ("3 weeks until Mother's Day") vs Subject C ("What's in our case right now"). Hypothesis: urgency wins for a conversion-stage email sent to engaged subscribers.

How to run in GHL: When creating each campaign, use the A/B split test feature. Set a 50/50 split. Send the test to 30% of the list first, wait 2 hours, then send the winner to the remaining 70%. Track open rates to determine the winner. Log results in performance-log.md for May planning.
