Trust Strategy

TRUST RECOVERY

Anyclean has 8,190 subscribers and a strong local reputation — 27 years in London, 4.7 stars on Google, 355 reviews. The problem isn't brand trust. It's email trust. Only 2 campaigns went out in the last 6 months, both in December 2025. Most of this list hasn't heard from you in months.

Anyclean has a large, dormant email list sitting on top of a healthy brand. The 58.6% open rate on those December sends is genuinely strong — it tells us the people who did see your emails actually wanted to read them. The issue is that only a fraction of the list received those campaigns. The rest have gone cold.

This month we're running a controlled reactivation. We start with the most engaged subscribers (December openers), prove to MailerLite and inbox providers that Anyclean sends emails people want, then gradually expand to less engaged segments. By the end of March, we'll know exactly how much of this list is recoverable — and spring is your busiest season, so the timing is right.

Diagnosis

WHAT WE FOUND

01

A dormant list with real potential

8,190 subscribers is a solid list for a London cleaning company. But only 2 emails went out in 6 months, both in December. That means roughly 95% of your list hasn't heard from you via email in over 3 months. They haven't unsubscribed — they've just been forgotten. That's recoverable, but it needs careful handling.

02

Strong opens, but tiny sample

58.6% open rate and 4% click rate from December are good numbers. But they came from a small segment. We can't assume those rates hold when we reach people who haven't seen an email in months. The reactivation ramp tests that assumption gradually instead of blasting the whole list and hoping for the best.

03

Zero automations running

No welcome sequence, no review requests, no win-back flow. Every new subscriber gets silence. Every finished job gets no follow-up. Every lapsed customer drifts away without a word. These are the easiest wins in email — set them up once and they run on their own, bringing in repeat bookings and reviews without manual effort.

04

Authentication status unknown

SPF, DKIM, and DMARC are all listed as "not sure" in the intake. For a list this size, that's a risk. If authentication isn't properly configured, emails can land in spam even when everything else is done right. This needs checking before any volume goes out.

Approach

THE PLAN FOR MARCH

01

Rewarm in tiers, not all at once

We start with your most engaged subscribers — the ~263 people who opened December's emails. Once inbox providers see those emails getting opened and clicked (not marked as spam), we expand to the next tier. Four tiers total, spread across the month. Each tier earns the right to send to the next one.

02

Lead with value, not sales

The first emails back after months of silence shouldn't ask for money. They should remind people why they signed up. A spring cleaning checklist they can actually use. A reintroduction to what Anyclean does. The booking ask comes later, after trust is re-established.

03

Launch three automations

Welcome sequence for new subscribers, review requests after completed jobs, and a win-back flow for people who go quiet. These run in the background permanently. They'll generate more repeat business and reviews than any single campaign.

04

Use spring to drive bookings

March through June is Anyclean's busiest season. End of tenancy cleaning, spring deep cleans, post-winter refreshes — demand is already there. The campaigns are timed to capture that seasonal intent, especially end of tenancy bookings where the stakes (deposit returns) make the decision straightforward.

Deliverability

SENDER HEALTH

Before sending anything at volume, these technical items need checking. MailerLite handles most of the heavy lifting. The action items below are specific to Anyclean's setup.

Likely in place

  • MailerLite handles bounce processing automatically
  • Unsubscribe links included by default on all sends
  • List-Unsubscribe header added by MailerLite
  • December open rates suggest reasonable inbox placement

Action required

  • Verify SPF, DKIM, and DMARC are configured for anyclean.co.uk — check in MailerLite under Settings → Domains
  • Set up a branded sending domain (e.g. mail.anyclean.co.uk) if not already active
  • Remove any hard bounces and unsubscribes from the list before March sends
  • Enable double opt-in on all new signup forms (GDPR compliance)
  • Check MailerLite's spam complaint rate — must stay below 0.1%

Testing Strategy

A/B Tests Built Into Every Send

Every campaign has subject line tests baked in. MailerLite makes this straightforward — you can split test subject lines and send the winner automatically.

01

Curiosity vs. Direct

Campaign 1 (Reintroduction): Subject A ("Remember us?") vs Subject B ("It's been a while, {$name}"). Does curiosity or personalised directness get more opens from a dormant list?

02

Seasonal Hook vs. Problem Hook

Campaign 2 (Spring Deep Clean): Subject A ("Spring cleaning season is here") vs Subject C ("{$name}, your home needs this before April"). Seasonal relevance against a direct problem statement.

03

Fear vs. Authority

Campaign 3 (End of Tenancy): Subject A ("Moving out? Read this first") vs Subject B ("{$name}, your deposit is at risk"). Does curiosity-based caution or direct pain get more opens?

04

Friendly vs. Specific

Welcome 01: Subject A ("Welcome, {$name}") vs Subject C ("{$name}, nice to meet you"). Testing warm-generic against warm-personal for first impressions.

How to run in MailerLite: When creating each campaign, enable "A/B split testing" in the campaign settings. Choose "Subject line" as the variable. Set a 50/50 split. Send to 30% of the audience first, wait 3 hours, then MailerLite automatically sends the winning subject to the remaining 70%. Track open rates. Log the winners in next month's performance review.
