What Each Email Does

Here's what each email covers and the subject line options we're considering for each one.

Regular Cleaning — Convert One-Offs to Recurring

"The Real Cost of Cleaning Yourself"

Tue 15 Apr

A gentle maths-based argument for why professional cleaning is actually cheaper than doing it yourself when you factor in time, products, and mental load. Ends with a soft quote CTA.

Subject lines: "What's your Saturday morning actually worth?" · "The real cost of cleaning your own home" · "You're spending more than you think on housework"

"Same Cleaner, Every Time"

Thu 17 Apr

Trust-building email focused on the consistency of having the same cleaner who gets to know your home, your preferences, and your routine. Not a hard sell — just makes people feel comfortable.

Subject lines: "You'll always get the same cleaner" · "Someone who actually knows your home" · "No strangers. Same face, every time."

Carpet & Upholstery — Drive Carpet Bookings

"What's Living in Your Carpet"

Tue 22 Apr

Pattern-interrupt opener about what actually accumulates in carpets over winter — dust mites, pollen, bacteria. Positions professional carpet cleaning as the proper spring reset. Mentions the hot water extraction method.

Subject lines: "What's actually living in your carpet right now" · "Your carpet has been through a long winter" · "The thing about carpets most people don't think about"

"Before & After: Real London Carpets"

Thu 24 Apr

Visual proof email — real before/after carpet photos from your team's work, paired with client testimonials. If photos aren't ready in time, we have a text-only fallback that leans on the reviews instead.

Subject lines: "Before and after: real carpets, real results" · "We let the photos do the talking" · "This is what a professional carpet clean actually does"

Bank Holiday Prep — May Long Weekend

"Bank Holiday Guests?"

Tue 29 Apr

Seasonal urgency email ahead of the Monday 5 May bank holiday. Lists every relevant service (regular clean, deep clean, carpet, end of tenancy) so readers can quickly find what applies. The angle is availability and timing, not discounts.

Subject lines: "Having people over for the bank holiday?" · "Bank holiday weekend. House sorted?" · "Slots are going before the long weekend"

Automations

New Systems Being Built This Month

These are automated email sequences that run in the background once activated. They trigger based on customer behaviour, so they work even when no one is manually sending emails.

Win-Back Sequence

Build Week: Apr 14

Reaches out to customers who haven't booked in a while with a friendly nudge to come back. Two emails, spaced a week apart, with a soft re-engagement angle.

2 emails · Automated · Triggers when a customer goes inactive

Cross-Sell Sequence

Build Week: Apr 21

After someone books one service, this sequence introduces them to related services they might not know about. Four different paths depending on which service they originally booked (regular cleaning, carpet, deep clean, end of tenancy).

8 emails (2 per service path) · Automated · Triggers after a completed job

Lapsed Customer Recovery

Build Week: Apr 28

Two separate sequences for residential and commercial customers who haven't booked in 90+ days. Different messaging for each — homeowners get a personal touch, commercial clients get a reliability and compliance angle.

6 emails (3 residential + 3 commercial) · Automated · Triggers at 90 days inactive
