01

Card specifications

Standard business card size: 85mm x 55mm. Matte or soft-touch finish. Print through Vistaprint, Moo, or a local printer. Order 500-1,000 to start. Cost: roughly 3-5p per card.

02

Generate the QR code

The QR code should link directly to your Google review page. To get this URL: search "Anyclean" on Google, click your business listing, click "Write a review," and copy that URL. Or use the Place ID method: https://search.google.com/local/writereview?placeid=YOUR_PLACE_ID. Use any free QR code generator to create the image.

03

Distribute to all 65 cleaners

Give each cleaner a stack of cards. Brief them: "After every job, leave one of these on the kitchen counter or somewhere the customer will see it. That's it. Don't ask them to scan it. Just leave it." The card does the asking for you.

04

Restock monthly

Track usage loosely. If 65 cleaners each do 3 jobs per week, that's ~780 cards per month. Order accordingly. At 4p per card, that's about 31 quid a month for a review generation machine.

Email Automation Improvements

Sharpen the Review Request Emails

The existing 2-email review automation is live but uses a placeholder review link. Here's what needs updating.

01

Get the actual Google review URL

Replace the placeholder link in Email 02 ("Quick Favour?") with the real Google review URL. Same URL as the QR code. This is the single most important action item in this document.

02

Make the review link a button, not a text link

In MailerLite, swap the plain hyperlink for a proper CTA button. Text: "Leave a Quick Review." Colour: match Anyclean's brand. A button gets 2-3x more clicks than a text link in email.

03

Add a one-line instruction

Above the button, add: "It takes about 30 seconds. Just click below, choose your star rating, and write a line or two." People are more likely to act when they know exactly how easy it is.

Review Response Strategy

Reply to Every Single Review

This is the easiest win in the whole system. Google's algorithm favours businesses that respond to reviews. It signals that you're active and engaged. And it shows potential customers that you care.

01

Reply within 24 hours

Set a daily habit. Someone on the office team checks Google reviews once a day (takes 5 minutes) and replies to any new ones. Speed matters: a quick reply feels personal.

02

Positive reviews: thank and personalise

Don't copy-paste the same "Thanks for your review!" reply. Mention something specific: the service type, the area, or something from the review. "Thanks Sarah, glad the end of tenancy clean went well. The flat in Clapham was a big job!" This takes 30 seconds and makes the reply feel real.

03

Negative reviews: respond publicly, resolve privately

If a negative review comes in, respond publicly with empathy: "We're sorry to hear this. We'd like to make it right. Please email us at [address] so we can sort this out." Never argue publicly. The public reply is for the hundreds of people who'll read it later, not just the reviewer.

04

Verbal ask training for cleaners

Brief the team with one simple line they can say at the end of a job: "If you're happy with the clean, a Google review really helps us." That's it. No script, no card-handing, no pressure. Just a genuine ask. Some cleaners will naturally do this more than others, and that's fine.
