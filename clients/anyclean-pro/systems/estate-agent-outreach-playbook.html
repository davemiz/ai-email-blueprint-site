Why This Matters

End of Tenancy Is Your Top Revenue Driver

End of tenancy cleaning is Anyclean's highest-value service. And the people who control the flow of that work? Estate agents and letting agents. They manage hundreds of tenancies across London. When a tenant moves out, the agent needs a reliable cleaning company. If you're their go-to, that's a recurring stream of high-value bookings you never have to advertise for.

Right now, these partnerships aren't being built systematically. This playbook gives your office team a repeatable process for reaching out, converting, and maintaining agent relationships.

This is a manual outreach system, not a MailerLite automation. Your office team sends these emails directly from your business email. The templates are ready to copy, personalise, and send. Focus on independent agencies first. The chains (Foxtons, Savills) have national contracts. Independent agents are more likely to partner with a local, trusted cleaning company.

Step 1

Build Your Target List

Start with 20-30 agents. Quality over quantity. Focus on agents in boroughs where Anyclean already operates and has strong reviews.

01

Search Google Maps

Search "estate agents [borough name]" or "letting agents [borough name]" for each area you serve. Look for independent agencies with decent Google reviews. Copy their name, email, phone, and the contact person's name (usually on their website's "About" or "Team" page).

02

Check Rightmove and Zoopla agent directories

Both platforms list agents by area. Filter by "letting agents" since they handle end of tenancy more directly. Cross-reference with your Google Maps list to fill gaps.

03

Prioritise boroughs with high tenant turnover

Focus on areas with lots of rental properties and frequent moves. Central and inner London boroughs tend to have higher turnover. Start with areas you're already cleaning in regularly.

  • High priority: Islington, Camden, Hackney, Southwark, Lambeth, Tower Hamlets, Wandsworth
  • Medium priority: Hammersmith, Kensington, Westminster, Lewisham, Greenwich
  • Expand from there based on results
04

Track in a simple spreadsheet

Columns: Agency Name, Contact Person, Email, Phone, Borough, Date Contacted, Response, Status (Not Contacted / Contacted / Interested / Active Partner). Review weekly.

Step 2

The Outreach Emails

Three emails, sent manually from your business email (not MailerLite). Personalise each one with the agent's name and borough. Space them 5-7 days apart.

Email 2 · New angle (Day 5-7)
Subject: inventory check headaches

Hi [Name],

The worst part of a tenancy changeover isn't finding a cleaner. It's when the clean doesn't pass inventory and you're chasing someone to come back.

We run dedicated end of tenancy teams. Every job comes with a re-clean guarantee, same-day availability if needed, and full insurance.

Do you have a preferred cleaning partner already?

[Your name]
Anyclean

Email 3 · Breakup (Day 10-14)
Subject: happy to be the backup

Hi [Name],

Last note from me. If you've got a cleaning company you're happy with, brilliant.

But if they ever cancel last minute or a clean doesn't pass checkout, we can usually turn it around same-day. 27 years in London, fully insured.

Worth keeping our number on file?

[Your name]
Anyclean
[Phone number]

Why these work: Each email is under 65 words. Estate agents get hundreds of cold emails. Short, specific, credible emails get read. Each touch uses a different angle (direct intro, pain point, backup positioning) so you're never repeating yourself. The low-case subject lines feel like a real person wrote them, not a marketing team.

Sending rules: Plain text from your business email. No MailerLite. No images or logos. Space 5-7 days apart. Stop after 3. If they don't reply after 3 touches, move on. Never chase.

Step 4

Keeping Partnerships Alive

Getting the first referral is the hard part. Keeping the relationship active is where the real revenue compounds.

01

After the first job: send a quality report

Email the agent after completing the first clean: "Hi [Name], just letting you know we've completed the end of tenancy clean at [address]. Everything looked great. Let me know if the tenant or landlord has any questions." This builds confidence that you're professional and communicative.

02

Monthly check-in (keep it brief)

Once a month, send a 2-line email: "Hi [Name], just checking in. Any properties coming up that need a clean? We've got availability this month and can usually turn things around in 48 hours. Let me know." No fluff. Just presence.

03

Quarterly review (for active partners)

For agents sending regular work, do a quick quarterly email: "Hi [Name], just wanted to say thanks for the continued work. We've done [X] cleans for your properties this quarter. Everything's gone smoothly. If there's anything we can improve, I'm all ears." This is about retention, not selling.

04

Seasonal push before peak months

In February and August (before the March-June and September-October peak moving seasons), send a heads-up: "Hi [Name], moving season is about to kick off. Just confirming we've got capacity for your properties. If you'd like priority scheduling, let me know and I'll block time." This positions Anyclean as proactive, not reactive.
