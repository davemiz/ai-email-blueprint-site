Why This Matters

Your Biggest Revenue Leak

Anyclean gets a steady flow of quote requests through the website. People fill in the form, receive a price, and then... nothing. No follow-up. No nudge. No social proof. No reminder. They drift to a competitor or simply forget.

This is the single biggest revenue leak in the business right now. Every quote that goes unfollowed is a potential booking lost to inaction.

This 4-email sequence fires automatically after someone requests a quote but doesn't book. It runs as a separate automation from the Welcome sequence, starting on Day 7 (two days after the Welcome sequence finishes on Day 5). The tone is warm, helpful, and never pushy. Each email addresses a different reason people hesitate: questions about the quote, trust concerns, and gentle urgency around availability.

MailerLite Setup

How to Build This

01

Create a new automation

In MailerLite, go to Automations and create a new workflow. Set the trigger to "When subscriber joins a group" and select the "Get a Quote Form" group. Add a 7-day delay as the first step. This ensures the Welcome sequence (which uses the same trigger) finishes first.

02

Add the 4 emails with delays

After the initial 7-day delay: add Email 01. Then add a 2-day delay, then Email 02. Then a 3-day delay, then Email 03. Then a 4-day delay, then Email 04. Total sequence runs from Day 7 to Day 16 after signup.

03

Set up the exit condition (optional but recommended)

Create a new group called "Booked" in MailerLite. Before each email step, add a condition: "If subscriber is NOT in group Booked, send email. Otherwise, skip." When someone books, manually add them to the "Booked" group. This stops follow-ups for people who've already converted.

04

Use merge tags and set send times

All emails use the {$name} merge tag. MailerLite will pull the subscriber's first name. If no name is stored, it defaults to blank (the emails are written to work with or without the name). Send times default to the delay timing, but if possible, set sends to land around 9:00 AM GMT for best open rates.

05

Activate and monitor

Once all 4 emails and delays are in place, activate the automation. It will only apply to new subscribers going forward (not existing ones). After the first week, check open rates and reply rates. If Email 01 gets below 20% opens, check your subject line and preview text.
