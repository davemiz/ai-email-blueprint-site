This Month's Trust Strategy
Month 1:

Build the Foundation

This is Month 1 — first time sending a consistent, strategically planned email program. The list is warm (people joined in-store during repairs) but email trust is low because sending has been inconsistent and generic. March is a trust-building month. All three campaigns are value-forward — no hard sells, no discounts. We're proving to subscribers that emails from Fast Fix are worth opening. Each location gets its own version highlighting what makes that store unique. The rhythm: educate → build credibility → soft seasonal ask. By the time we ask for anything, we've already deposited twice.

Account Diagnosis

Where Things Stand

01

List Health

~8,512 active subscribers across 4 locations. Engagement rates vary widely — Barton Creek 10.89% active, Sarasota 5.80%, KOP 11.22%, St. Matthews 15.27%. Segment to Active 90 days for all sends. Do not blast full lists.

02

Deliverability

Open rates averaging 10-15% are below where they should be. Likely caused by inconsistent sending history and sending to unengaged contacts. Sending only to active contacts will immediately improve open rates and sender reputation.

03

Current Automations

None. No welcome sequence, no post-repair follow-up, no review request flow. Automations are included in this blueprint (Welcome + Post-Repair + Review Request). Campaigns launch first.

04

Growth Opportunity

Fine jewelry case sales are a stated growth priority. Most customers come for repairs but don't browse retail pieces. Campaign 3 (Mother's Day) subtly introduces fine jewelry without hard-selling it.

Monthly Strategy

The March Approach

01

Primary Goal

Establish Fast Fix as a valuable presence in subscribers' inboxes. Build email trust before asking for anything. Drive incremental foot traffic to all 4 locations.

02

Trust Architecture

Two value deposits before one soft ask. Campaign 1 educates, Campaign 2 builds credibility, Campaign 3 makes a gentle seasonal suggestion. No discounts. No urgency. Just helpfulness.

03

Segmentation

Each location sends separately using their own contact list. Send only to Active 90 per location. This means 4 separate sends per campaign — each with location-specific content, address, and unique angles.

04

Key Metrics

Target open rate: 25%+ (up from 10-15%). Target click rate: 3%+. Unsubscribe ceiling: 0.3% per send. If we hit these on Active 90 sends, we expand the audience next month.

Testing Strategy

A/B Tests Built Into Every Send

Every campaign has A/B subject line tests baked in. Here's the testing framework — GHL makes this easy with built-in split testing.

01

Urgency vs. Specificity

Campaign 1 — Barton Creek: Subject A (seasonal hook) vs Subject C (specific service list). Hypothesis: specificity wins for a first email from a brand they barely know.

02

Social Proof vs. Speed

Campaign 2 — Barton Creek: Subject A (review count) vs Subject C (while-you-wait). Hypothesis: social proof wins for credibility-stage emails.

03

Date Urgency vs. Personalization

Campaign 3 — King of Prussia: Subject A (6 weeks away) vs Subject D ({{contact.first_name}}, her favorite piece). Hypothesis: personalization beats generic urgency.

04

Quote vs. Brand Phrase

Campaign 2 — St. Matthews: Subject A (customer quote) vs Subject C (brand phrase). Hypothesis: customer quote wins at this trust stage — but St. Matthews only has 12 reviews, so the "honest" angle may resonate more than numbers.

How to run in GHL: When creating each campaign, use the A/B split test feature. Set a 50/50 split. Send the test to 30% of the list first, wait 2 hours, then send the winner to the remaining 70%. Track open rates to determine the winner. Log results for next month's planning.
