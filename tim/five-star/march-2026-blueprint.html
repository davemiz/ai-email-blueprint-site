Account Diagnosis

Where Things Stand

01

List Health Crisis

143,279 total subscribers but only 12,799 are active — that's 7.59%. Over 130,000 contacts are dead weight, dragging down sender reputation with every send. Suppress all contacts with no engagement in 180+ days immediately. Do not send to the full list under any circumstances.

02

Engagement Emergency

7.71% open rate and 0.07% click rate. Industry average for jewelry is 15-20% opens and 1-2% clicks. Five Star is running at less than half the baseline. Sending only to active contacts will immediately improve these numbers and begin rebuilding sender reputation.

03

Content Gap

The only email content has been "We Buy Gold" messages — pure trust withdrawals. No educational content, no storytelling, no product showcases, no seasonal relevance. Every send has been an ask with nothing given in return. March shifts to value-first content: care guides, social proof, and soft seasonal previews.

04

Automation Void

Zero automations running. No welcome sequence, no post-purchase follow-up, no review request flow. Every new customer gets nothing after their purchase. This blueprint includes 3 automation flows (Welcome + Post-Purchase + Review Request) to deploy alongside campaigns.

Deliverability

Sender Reputation Recovery & Warmup Ramp

Your sender reputation is likely damaged from months of blasting 143K contacts with sub-8% open rates. Even though you're reducing volume, you need a structured ramp to rebuild trust with inbox providers. Do not skip this — sending to the full active list on day one risks landing in spam.

01

Week 1 — Seed Send (Most Engaged Only)

Create a "Super Active" smart list: contacts who opened or clicked in the last 30 days. This is your warmup seed — likely 3,000–5,000 contacts. Send Campaign 1 (Spring Jewelry Care Guide) to this group only. Target: 35%+ open rate. High engagement signals to Gmail/Yahoo/Outlook that your emails are wanted. If opens land below 25%, do NOT expand volume — diagnose subject lines and content first.

02

Week 2 — Expand to Full Active List

If Week 1 opens are 25%+, expand to the full "Active 90 Days" list (~12,800 contacts). Send Campaign 2 (Why Miami Families Choose Five Star). Monitor bounce rate — if hard bounces exceed 2%, pause and clean the list. Watch spam complaints in GHL — anything above 0.1% is a red flag. Reply-to should be monitored — real replies improve sender reputation faster than anything else.

03

Week 3–4 — Steady State + Monitor

Continue sending to the full active list. By now inbox providers should be recalibrating your sender reputation based on the new, healthier engagement signals. Key metrics to watch weekly: open rate (target 25%+), click rate (target 2%+), bounce rate (keep under 1%), spam complaint rate (keep under 0.08%). If all metrics are healthy by end of Month 1, you're cleared for normal cadence in April.

04

Ongoing — Deliverability Hygiene

Monthly: Run a GHL deliverability check — verify SPF/DKIM/DMARC are still passing, check blacklist status, review bounce trends. Every 90 days: Re-evaluate the "Active" window — contacts who haven't engaged in 90+ days get moved to suppression. Never: Re-add suppressed contacts to the active list without a dedicated re-engagement campaign (planned for Month 2+). Treat the suppression list as permanent unless proven otherwise.

Living in Miami means your jewelry takes a beating you probably don't notice.

Sunscreen, salt air, pool chlorine, humidity — they all wear on your pieces over time. Prongs loosen, stones dull, gold loses its shine. Most people don't realize until something falls out or a clasp breaks.

Testing Strategy

A/B Tests Built Into Every Send

Every campaign has A/B subject line tests baked in. Here's the testing framework for March.

01

Pain Point vs. Free Offer

Campaign 1: Subject A ("Miami is hard on your jewelry") vs Subject C ("Free cleaning — any Five Star location"). Hypothesis: the pain point hook wins for re-engaging a burned list because it creates curiosity, not a sales signal.

02

Emotional vs. Factual

Campaign 2: Subject A ("Why families keep coming back") vs Subject C ("8 locations, still family-owned"). Hypothesis: emotional hook wins for credibility-stage emails — people connect with "why" more than facts.

03

Curiosity vs. Specificity

Campaign 3: Subject A ("New pieces just arrived") vs Subject C ("Lab-grown diamonds starting under $1K"). Hypothesis: specificity wins for consideration-stage emails — people who've opened two emails want concrete details, not vague curiosity.

04

English vs. Spanish Subject Lines

Cross-campaign: Compare open rates of English Monday sends vs Spanish Tuesday sends. If Spanish consistently outperforms on the Spanish segment, consider Spanish-first subject lines with English body copy for bilingual contacts.

How to run in GHL: When creating each campaign, use the A/B split test feature. Set a 50/50 split. Send the test to 30% of the list first, wait 2 hours, then send the winner to the remaining 70%. Track open rates to determine the winner. Log results for April's planning.

Follow this order. Don't skip steps. Each week builds on the last.

Week of March 10

Triage the list. Create the "Unengaged 180+ Days" smart list and suppress it from all sends. Create "Active 90 Days" smart list (all, English, Spanish). Create language preference custom field. Tag contacts you know speak Spanish. Set up automation workflows in GHL (don't activate yet).

Week of March 17

Send Campaign 1: Spring Jewelry Care Guide. English on Monday, Spanish on Tuesday. Copy email bodies from this blueprint. Set up A/B subject line tests. Send to Active 90 Days list only. Activate Welcome Sequence automation. Activate Post-Purchase automation.

Week of March 24

Send Campaign 2: Why Miami Families Choose Five Star. English Monday, Spanish Tuesday. Review Campaign 1 open/click rates. Adjust if needed. Activate Review Request automation.

Week of March 31

Send Campaign 3: Spring Collection Preview. English Monday, Spanish Tuesday. First soft conversion ask. Review all March metrics. Begin planning April campaigns based on what worked. Start building behavioral segments from March click data.
