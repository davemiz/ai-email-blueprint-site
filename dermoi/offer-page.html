01
Escalation Too Fast

Your Welcome Series is asking for a purchase before it's earned the right to.

ETA maps every subscriber to one of seven trust stages. A new subscriber who opts in through an ad or quiz is at Stage 2: Interested. Curious. Cautious. No prior purchase history. Not ready to buy.

Your welcome sequence has an RPR of £0.69 — which sounds reasonable until you look at the trust stage mismatch. The sequence moves from introduction to purchase CTA without passing through the Engaged stage. It's asking for Buyer trust at Stage 2 interest levels.

ETA's Rule of Adjacency requires every email to move the reader only one stage forward. Interested → Engaged. Engaged → Buyer. Skipping stages isn't aggressive marketing. It's trust withdrawal from an account that hasn't been funded.

Your abandoned cart flows perform at £1-5 RPR because those readers arrived at Stage 3-4 on their own — they showed purchase intent organically. The welcome series collapses because it assumes Stage 4 trust in a Stage 2 reader.

02
Emotional Intensity Too High

96% of your campaigns use urgency or emoji-first subjects. Your audience has tuned them out.

ETA tracks emotional calibration across your send history. Across 91 campaigns, 96% of your subject lines open with emoji or urgency language. Variations of "don't miss this," "final invitation," "ends tonight," and "last chance" make up a third of all broadcast subjects.

The problem isn't urgency. Urgency works when it's earned. When every email is urgent, nothing is. Your list has been conditioned to treat urgency as ambient noise rather than a buying signal.

ETA's subject line approach is built around specific curiosity, not generic pressure. Short, precise subjects paired with preview text that completes the thought. Curiosity converts. Pressure habituates.

Your "Because You Opened" flow — sent to people who opened but didn't click — generates just £0.03 RPR because it sends purchase CTAs to Stage 2 readers with no added proof, no emotional bridge, and maximum urgency. High emotional intensity to someone who already said "not yet" creates trust debt, not conversions.

Urgency used on everyone is urgency felt by no one.
Certainty is what converts. Urgency just accelerates it — if the trust is already there.

03
Identity Misalignment

Post-purchase RPR is £0.15. Your buyers aren't being confirmed as buyers before the next ask arrives.

ETA's post-purchase diagnostic shows a classic identity misalignment. When someone makes their first purchase from dermoi, they're at Stage 4: Buyer. Their dominant emotional state is a mix of excitement and latent doubt — did I make the right choice? Will this actually work for my skin?

The post-purchase flow's job at this stage is identity reinforcement. Not upsell. Not cross-sell. The first sequence after purchase must confirm the buyer's identity: "You made a smart call. Here's what to expect. Here's how to get the most from what you just bought."

Buyer's remorse unaddressed becomes buyer's hesitation on the next order. That's why your post-purchase RPR is £0.15 against a £1-5 RPR benchmark for well-architected post-purchase flows. The sequence is asking for Repeat Buyer trust before Stage 4 buyer identity is even confirmed.

Fifteen flows currently live in your Klaviyo account generate zero revenue. Browse abandonment, winback, loyalty — all structurally misaligned from the first email. The audience targeting is correct. The trust stage framing is not.

The ETA Solution

It's not a copy problem.
It's a trust architecture problem.

Email Trust Architecture maps every Klaviyo subscriber to a trust stage and builds the system to move them forward, one stage at a time.

Your list opens. That trust is real. The architecture just doesn't know what to do with it. Every sequence currently pushes too hard, too fast, with too much urgency and not enough certainty.

ETA defines 7 trust stages. Each requires different content, different CTAs, and different emotional framing. Right now, your sequences treat a first-time subscriber the same as a repeat buyer. That's why the revenue gap exists.

7 Trust Stages.
7 Different Architectures.

Stage 1: Unaware
Reached via ad or social. Needs a soft opt-in sequence that introduces the dermoi difference — not a discount.
Stage 2: Interested
Subscribed but hasn't bought. Needs trust deposits: education, proof, ingredient science. No purchase ask yet.
Stage 3: Engaged
Opens regularly, clicks occasionally. Needs the G/L/F 3-email campaign arc with adjacency-compliant purchase CTAs.
Stage 4: Buyer
First purchase made. Needs identity confirmation and usage momentum before any repeat-buy ask arrives.
Stage 5: Repeat Buyer
Multiple purchases. High trust. Earns VIP treatment, early access, and higher-value cross-sell offers.
Stage 6: Advocate
Refers friends, leaves reviews. Needs referral automation, review flows, UGC sequences, and community belonging.
Stage 7: Influencer
Active promoter in their network. VIP status, affiliate pathway, exclusive access, brand ambassador sequences.

Right now, a new subscriber who clicked a Facebook ad gets the same urgency-driven email as a repeat buyer. That's trust stage compression — and it's responsible for the gap between your open rate and your revenue.

ETA fixes this by mapping every contact to their current stage and running stage-specific sequences automatically through Klaviyo.
